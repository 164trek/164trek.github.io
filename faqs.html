FAQs

Good to know

Simple answers for the things people usually wonder about before joining a multi-day trek.

1. How fit do I need to be? What if I’m worried I’m not fit enough?

You don't need to be an athlete, but you do need to be honest about your current fitness. Multi-day trekking means long hours on your feet, uneven ground, stairs, heat, cold, and tired legs. If you're unsure, join a few weekend training hikes first and see how your body handles it.

2. How should I train for a multi-day trek from Singapore?

Singapore doesn't have big mountains, so training is mostly about consistency. Long walks, stairs, Bukit Timah, MacRitchie, loaded backpack walks, and steady weekend hikes are all useful. The free weekend training hikes are open to anyone in the community, even if you're not sure about joining a trip yet.

3. What is AMS, and how do I prepare for it?

AMS means acute mountain sickness, which can happen when your body struggles with altitude. Fitness helps, but pace matters more. Some of the fittest people get hit hardest because they move too fast. Diamox can be prescribed by GPs in Singapore for altitude prevention, but you should ask a doctor before taking it.

4. What gear do I actually need? Do I need expensive boots and poles?

You don't need the most expensive gear, but you do need gear that's tested before the trip. Decathlon Quechua boots are fine if you break them in for at least 50 km first. For loose scree terrain, two trekking poles are essential, not one.

5. What if something goes wrong on the trek: injury, weather, or AMS?

We work with licensed local guides on every multi-day trek, and their judgment comes first on the mountain. If someone gets injured, the weather turns bad, or AMS symptoms get serious, the plan may change. Getting everyone down safely matters more than reaching the summit.

6. What if I need to drop out after paying?

Tell us as early as possible. We pay the local guide a single package fee that covers most of the trip costs, so refunds depend on how close we are to departure and how much of that fee has already been sent. If costs are already locked in, we show you exactly what was spent and what can still be recovered. We don't keep any money you've paid. Anything unused is returned.

7. What if I’m joining solo and don’t know anyone in the group?

That's completely fine. A lot of people join these kinds of trips because they want to meet others who like hiking, training, and doing something a bit harder than a normal holiday. We keep the group small so people can get comfortable before the trip.

8. Where does my money actually go?

We publish a full cost breakdown for each trip. Most of it goes to the local guide package (hotels, transfers, guide, porters, meals, permits, snorkeling, etc.). A small buffer covers unexpected costs and is refunded or rolled into the next trek if unused. We don't keep a margin. Every penny is tracked.
