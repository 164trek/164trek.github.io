FAQs

Good to know

Simple answers for the things people usually wonder about before joining a 164 Trek trip.

1. How fit do I need to be? What if I’m worried I’m not fit enough?

You don't need to be an athlete, but you do need to be honest about your current fitness. Different treks can involve long hours on your feet, uneven ground, heat, cold, rain, stairs, river crossings, altitude, or tired legs. If you're unsure, join a few weekend training hikes first and see how your body handles it.

2. How should I train for a multi-day trek from Singapore?

Singapore doesn't have big mountains, so training is mostly about consistency. Long walks, stairs, Bukit Timah, MacRitchie, loaded backpack walks, and steady weekend hikes are all useful. The free weekend training hikes are open to anyone in the community, even if you're not sure about joining a trip yet.

3. What is AMS, and does it apply to every trek?

AMS means acute mountain sickness, which can happen when your body struggles with altitude. It mainly applies to altitude treks, not every low mountain, jungle trek, or warm-up hike. Fitness helps, but pace, acclimatization, sleep, hydration, and listening to symptoms matter more.

For future altitude treks, some hikers speak with a doctor about prevention options such as Diamox. Please get proper medical advice before taking any medication.

4. What gear do I actually need? Do I need expensive boots and poles?

You don't need the most expensive gear, but you do need gear that's suitable for the specific trek and tested before the trip. Jungle treks, cold mountains, wet trails, camping routes, and altitude treks can require different shoes, layers, rain protection, sleep systems, and personal medication.

We will share a trip-specific packing guide once the route and guide requirements are confirmed.

5. What happens if there is injury, bad weather, exhaustion, or altitude issues?

We work with local guides or operators for multi-day treks, and their safety judgment comes first on the trail. If someone gets injured, the weather turns bad, the group is exhausted, or altitude symptoms become serious, the plan may change.

Getting everyone down safely matters more than reaching a summit or completing the original itinerary.

6. What if I need to drop out after paying?

Tell us as early as possible. Some costs may already be committed to the local guide/operator, permits, transport, accommodation, or other arrangements. Any refundable amount will depend on what has already been paid and what can still be recovered. We'll share the cancellation terms before you pay so there are no surprises.

As a rule of thumb: the closer to departure, the less is recoverable, because flights, permits, and operator deposits get locked in along the way. We don't keep any money that isn't already spent.

7. What if I’m joining solo and don’t know anyone in the group?

That's completely fine. A lot of people join these kinds of trips because they want to meet others who like hiking, training, and doing something a bit harder than a normal holiday. We keep the group small so people can get comfortable before the trip.

8. Where does my money actually go?

Costs are shared clearly for each trip once the guide/operator, route, permits, transport, and group size are known. Most of the money goes to local guide or operator fees, permits, transport, accommodation, food arrangements, and other ground costs.

If a buffer is collected and not used, we explain it clearly to participants.

9. What if the group doesn't reach minimum size?

Some trips need a minimum group size because guide, transport, permit, or accommodation costs are shared. If the group does not reach the minimum size, the trip may be postponed, repriced, or cancelled. We will explain the options clearly before collecting payment.

10. Are all trips beginner-friendly?

No. Some trips are suitable for newer hikers who train consistently, while others may involve jungle terrain, long trekking days, technical weather, remote camps, or high altitude. We will describe the expected difficulty before asking anyone to commit.

11. What are the challenges of different treks?

Low mountains may still be hot, steep, muddy, or slippery. Jungle treks can be wet, humid, and mentally tiring. High mountains can involve cold, long days, thinner air, or altitude sickness. Overseas treks may also involve travel delays, changing weather, and unfamiliar food or sleeping conditions.

12. Do I need to pay immediately?

Please message us first before sending any payment. Final payment details, PayNow QR, and Wise payment link will be shared clearly before collection. After payment, send us a screenshot or receipt so we can verify and confirm your slot.
