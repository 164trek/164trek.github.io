FAQs

Good to know

General answers that apply across our trips, from low mountains and jungle treks to high mountains and altitude routes. Trip-specific details are shared on each trip page and before payment.

1. How fit do I need to be?

It depends on the trek. A short hike may only need steady basic fitness, while a multi-day jungle or mountain trek can mean long hours, steep ground, a loaded pack, heat, cold, and tired legs. We describe the expected difficulty for each trip. If you are unsure, join a few training hikes first and see how your body responds.

2. How should I train from Singapore?

Consistency matters more than finding a big mountain. Long walks, stairs, Bukit Timah, MacRitchie, loaded-backpack walks, strength work, and steady weekend hikes all help. Build up so your training gradually matches the trek's distance, elevation, terrain, and pack weight.

3. What is AMS, and does it apply to every trek?

AMS means acute mountain sickness. It is relevant to altitude treks, not every trek. Low mountains and most jungle treks do not carry the same altitude risk. For a high-altitude trip we will discuss the ascent rate, acclimatisation, symptoms, and turnaround plan in advance. Always ask a doctor for personal medical advice or before taking altitude medication.

4. What are the main challenges on different treks?

Low mountains can still be steep and hot. Jungle treks may be muddy, wet, humid, slippery, and remote, with river crossings and leeches. High mountains add cold, wind, and exposed terrain. Altitude treks also bring acclimatisation and AMS concerns. Each trip page explains the main challenges for that route.

5. What gear do I need?

It changes with the route, weather, altitude, and how you sleep on the trek. You do not always need expensive gear, but your footwear, rain protection, layers, and pack should fit properly and be tested before departure. We share a trip-specific checklist once details are set.

6. What happens if there is injury, bad weather, or exhaustion?

The plan may change, turn around, or stop. On the mountain, the licensed guide's safety judgment comes first. The response depends on the trek and may include rest, descent, evacuation, or outside help. Reaching the summit or finishing the route never matters more than getting everyone back safely.

7. Where does my money go?

Costs vary by trip and may include the guide or operator, permits, transport, accommodation or camping, food, support crew, and other shared logistics. The known costs, assumptions, and any buffer are shown clearly before payment is requested.

8. Do I need to pay right away?

No. Please message us first before sending any payment. We confirm your slot, deposit amount, and cancellation terms before you reserve. Final payment details are shared clearly before collection.

9. What if I drop out after paying?

Tell us as early as possible. Some costs may already be committed to guides, operators, permits, accommodation, or transport. Any refund depends on the trip's stated cancellation terms and what can still be recovered. Those terms are shared before any payment is requested.

10. What if the group does not reach the minimum size?

Group cost per person depends on how many people join, and some treks have a minimum size set by guide, permit, and transport arrangements. We state the minimum size and cutoff before asking for payment. If a trip cannot proceed, the cancellation and refund terms for that trip apply.

11. Can I join solo, and are all trips beginner-friendly?

Joining solo is completely fine. Many people join to meet others who enjoy hiking and training, and we keep groups small. Not every trip is beginner-friendly though. Some suit beginners who train, while others need prior multi-day, remote, or altitude experience. We describe the expected fitness and experience for each trip so you can make an honest decision.
